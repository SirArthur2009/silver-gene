Reproducing the Silver Gene

The silver gene requires two qualifications to reproduce. One, a Labrador with two copies of the silver gene. Two, Labrador with at least one copy of the gene. Once this is met, they can breed and reproduce puppies with the silver gene. The yellow labrador can carry copies of the silver gene but since they are already a lighter color, it doesn't show.

What the silver gene really is

The silver gene is actually a mutation in the gene. This mutation results in a lighter, or almost discoloration. It does not really take away from the dog's health, and is handsome to behold. Therefore the silver gene is sought after and reproduced. The reason why the AKC does not recognize the silver as a color is because, is because it is actually a chocolate. Without the genetic altering it would be a chocolate. Charcoal, in a similer way, are actually blacks. Without the genetic difference, they would be the 'base' color, therefore not actually being there 'own' color

Possibilties

The actually gene is symbolizes by Dd for one copy of silver and dd for two. DD is no copies of silver. To make it easier to understand, I will now refer to those as named.
When a dog with Dd and a dog with Dd breed, you will not get any silver colored dogs. With a dd and Dd, you will get equal Possibilties for silver, charcoal, and the base color(s) of the parents. A dog with dd and dd with breed all silvers and charcoals considering both parents have two copies of the silver gene.
